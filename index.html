Instructions:

Enter any gmail address in the box above and click 'Generate', this will produce aliases for that email address by randomly placing periods before the @ and randomising gmail/googlemail. Keep pressing the button, or change the dropdown to get more aliases

When is this useful?

This is useful any time you need a 'unique' email, but don't want to create a whole new email address, for example:

  • Testing - need 100 fresh emails to test the new account creation journey on your app? Use some of these and save yourself creating 99 actual email addresses.
  • Security - if you use an alias of an email for a service that is then hacked, that email won't be the same email you've used for other services, making it harder to hack you.
  • Privacy - using different emails make you harder to track.

FAQs

Q - Can't I just use a + and then add any text I like?

A - Sometimes! This is easy to do, for example myemail+1@gmail.com myemail+2@gmail.com etc, but I have found that '+' is often not an allowed character in email signup forms, whereas '.' always is. Consecutive '.'s I don't think always work, so tend to stick to one '.'

Q - Are these alias' always unique?

A - No, they're always random, but there is a chance of getting one you've had before (the longer the email the lower the chance)
